Be part of a community garden

A community garden is defined and described by the American Community Garden
Association (the ACGA) as a place. that can be urban, suburban, or rural. It can grow
flowers, vegetables or community. It can be one community plot, or can be many individual
plots. It can be at a school, hospital, or in a neighborhood.

Why would anyone want to do this?

Many people in our communities do not have access to food, especially fresh produce. Due to
high cost, or being located in a food desert, access is not always readily available. By starting,
or being a part of, a community garden you provide opportunities for education, nutrition, therapy,
relationship building, skill development and bridging gaps between those in the community.

The Environmental Evidence Journal did a study on the impact of community gardens.

In most areas, there are vegetable gardens, pollinator gardens (gardens with flowering plants to
attract pollinators like birds, bees, butterfiles etc) or a combination of both. Each will have a
Master Gardener associated or someone in charge that tends the garden. We connect you with them. All
levels of experience, from none to expert, and welcome and encouraged to participate.

How to get involved

Using your talents, skills and/or experience you can participate in:

    -Planning the garden year
    -Delivery of the produce to local locations
    -Tending the garden
    -Keeping records in a book log and blog
    -Organizing events around the garden
